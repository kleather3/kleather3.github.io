Different Breeds of Guinea Pig

There are 13 different breeds of guinea pig, most of the special breeds are not kept as pets so you may never have heard of some of them 

 1) Abyssinian Guinea Pig 

These guinea pigs have permanent be head due to the cowlicks in their fur all over their bodies, they are also very excitable. 

2) American Guinea Pig 

This is the most common type of guinea pig and comes in nineteen different colours. They are very sweet and docile hence why they make such popular pets!

3) Peruvian Guinea Pig 

These guys have insane hair it can grow up to two feet long making the Abyssinian’s bedhead look tidy! They are very alert and inquisitive animals.

4) Sheltie/Silkie Guinea Pig 

Like the Peruvians these little critters have crazy hair but it’s a lot tidier as it only grows in one direction, backwards. They are very good pets for family with small children.

5) Sheba Guinea Pigs 

These little piggies are awesome they are very well known for their muttonchop facial hair that frames their face. This breed comes from Australia!

6) Coronet Guinea Pig 

This type also have long flowing hair like the Sheltie and are very demanding for attention from their owners.

7) Lunkarya 

This breed comes from Sweden and has a long curly coat that makes it look like a mop not a Guinea Pig! They are also affectionately known as Lunks! 

8) Rex Guinea Pig 

These guys have very short fluffy hair unlike the other breed and love cuddles and being handled. 

9) Baldwin Guinea Pig 

Baldwin’s are born with facial hair but no other hair on their bodies. They shed the facial hair until only a few whiskers remain. This makes them look like tiny hippos. They love the warmth but need an experienced owner. 

10) Alpaca Guinea Pigs 

These little piggies have very thick and rough hair that is easily tangled. They require daily brushing!!! 

11) Texel Guinea Pigs 

These guys have super dense wavy fur that also needs daily maintenance. They can be a bit beastly so are less commonly bred as pets. 

12) Teddy Guinea Pigs 

It’s in the name! These guys look like really fluffy teddies and are loving and playful. They make great pets and love spending time with other guinea pigs. 

13) Skinny Guinea Pigs 

These little piggies are very similar to the Baldwin Guinea Pig and its because of all that hairless skin that they need a lot of maintenance. They are prone to skin infections and cuts and need an experienced owner.
